Introduction!

What To Know About The Summer Season

Summer, the season of warmth and vitality, unfolds with longer days, higher temperatures, and a burst of vibrant life. It is a time when nature is in full bloom, offering a plethora of outdoor activities and bountiful harvests.

What months are considered Summer?

Months of Summer

In the Northern Hemisphere:

  • June
  • July
  • August

    • In the Southern Hemisphere:

  • December
  • January
  • February

    • How do we know the Summer season?

    Characteristics of Summer

  • Warm to Hot Temperatures: Summer brings an increase in temperatures, creating a comfortable and often hot environment.
  • Long Days and Shorter Nights: The summer solstice marks the longest day of the year, providing ample daylight for various activities.
  • Peak of Sunlight:Plants experience active growth and blooming, reaching their peak during the summer months.
  • Outdoor Festivities: The season is marked by outdoor events, festivals, and recreational activities.

    • Farming practices that can happen during Summer season?

    Activities Carried Out In Summer

    Outdoor Farming: Active cultivation and irrigation of crops.

    Beach Vacations and Water Sports: Coastal areas become popular destinations.

    Harvest Festivals: Celebrations of abundant summer crops.

    Gardening: Tending to home gardens and enjoying the colors of blooming flowers.

    Conclusion

    In Summary

    Summer, a season of warmth and activity, invites people to bask in the sun's rays and revel in the vibrancy of nature. Farmers engage in bountiful harvests, while communities come together to celebrate the joys of summer through outdoor events and festivals.
